In Loving Memory of

Pastor Innocent Hungwe

Left us November 1, 2025 | A Servant of the Gospel

“Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted.” — Matthew 5:4

It is with heavy hearts that we announce the passing of our beloved Pastor Innocent Hungwe. He was a dedicated shepherd, a faithful servant, and a man whose life was a testament to the principles of the Seventh-day Adventist Church. His commitment to God’s Word and his anticipation of the Second Coming of Christ were the foundations of his ministry and a true legacy in the world of preaching and working for God.

A Grandson's Farewell

My dearest Grandfather, Pastor Innocent Hungwe, there are no words to truly capture the void your passing has left in my heart. I wanted to build this website for you while you were still here—to celebrate your ministry, to archive your sermons, and to show the world the depth of your faith and your gentle soul. I wanted to show you how much I adored you.

Building it now, in your honor, is the hardest and most heartfelt thing I have ever done. Every line of code, every uploaded picture, is a tear and a memory shared. Thank you for everything you taught me about faith, integrity, and the blessed hope. I loved you wholeheartedly, and your legacy will never fade.

— Your Grandson, Hillary Mpofu

A Life of Ministry and Prophecy (The Three Angels' Messages)

Pastor Hungwe dedicated his life to sharing the Three Angels' Messages with passion and conviction. He often quoted the foundational text of his ministry:

"Then I saw another angel flying in the midst of heaven, **having the everlasting gospel to preach to those who dwell on the earth**—to every nation, tribe, tongue, and people—saying with a loud voice, 'Fear God and give glory to Him, for the hour of His judgment has come; and worship Him who made heaven and earth, the sea and springs of water.'" — **Revelation 14:6-7**

He was a profound student of Scripture, focusing on the end-time prophecies, including the **Sabbath** and the **Mark of the Beast**. His voice resonated with truth, guiding many to a deeper understanding of God's plan. He lived by the faith of Jesus, always reminding us:

"Here is the patience of the saints; here are those who keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus." — **Revelation 14:12**

He held a deep and abiding love for the writings of **Ellen G. White**, which he often referred to as a "lesser light leading to the greater light." His sermons were frequently illuminated by her counsels, particularly from The Great Controversy and Steps to Christ. His legacy will live on through the countless souls he led to a deeper relationship with Jesus and through the profound truths he shared from the pulpit.
